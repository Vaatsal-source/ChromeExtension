SLIDE 2 — INTRODUCTION + OVERVIEW

- Guiding, not forcing

Why It Matters:
- Useful in leadership, business, and daily life

Core Idea:

Topics Covered:
- Psychology of persuasion
- Clarity & structure
- Emotion, credibility, logic
- Delivery techniques

SLIDE 3 — PSYCHOLOGY OF PERSUASION
How People Are Persuaded:
- Emotion + Logic + Trust
- First impressions matter

AIDA Model:
- Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

Key Insight:
- Stories are remembered more than facts

Match your message to how people think and feel

----------------------------------------

SLIDE 4 — CLARITY & STRUCTURE
Less is More:
- Simple, focused ideas
- Avoid overload

Clear Flow:
- Beginning → Middle → End
- One idea per section

Tips:
- Short sentences
- Use examples
- Repeat key points

Clarity = Stronger persuasion

----------------------------------------

SLIDE 5 — EMOTION + TRUST (PATHOS + ETHOS)
Emotional Connection (Pathos):
- Use storytelling
- Real-life examples
- Expressive tone

Credibility (Ethos):
- Show knowledge & preparation
- Be confident and authentic

People act on feelings and believe those they trust

----------------------------------------

SLIDE 6 — LOGIC + REASONING (LOGOS)
Use Logic Effectively:
- Facts, data, and evidence
- Clear arguments

Structure:
- Claim → Evidence → Explanation

Avoid:
- Weak or confusing points

Strong logic strengthens persuasion

----------------------------------------

SLIDE 7 — DELIVERY TECHNIQUES
Voice:
- Clear, loud, varied tone

Body Language:
- Eye contact
- Gestures
- Confident posture

Engagement:
- Ask questions
- Use pauses

Delivery brings your message to life
